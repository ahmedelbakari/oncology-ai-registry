Vesta Bladder BCGPredict

Valar Labs
Histopathology bladder

Predicts patient response to intravesical BCG immunotherapy in high-risk non-muscle-invasive bladder cancer (NMIBC) from H&E whole-slide images.

Validation summary

Study designRetrospective
Cohort size944 patients — patients (development cohort 303 across 5 centers; validation cohort 641 across 7 centers); slide count not enumerated in the publication
Number of sites12
Site geographyMulti-center (international)
ComparatorClinicopathologic factors
Primary endpointhazard_ratio
Primary resultInternational validation cohort (n=641, 7 centers): high-grade recurrence HR 2.08 (95% CI 1.80–2.40, P<.0001); progression to muscle invasion HR 3.87 (95% CI 2.75–5.44, P<.001); cystectomy HR 3.35 (95% CI 2.51–4.47, P<.001); BCG-unresponsive disease HR 2.31 (95% CI 1.89–2.82, P<.0001). Median follow-up 36 months.
External validationYes — International external validation cohort: 641 patients across 7 centers in the United States, Australia, Belgium, Netherlands, and Chile, distinct from the 303-patient 5-center development cohort. · AI assays provided predictive information beyond clinicopathologic factors; HRs reported above achieved in the external validation cohort.

Key publications

    Journal of Urology · 2025

Limitations noted

Publication notes that optimal validation would use a prospective clinical trial to mitigate confounding and bias; BCG regimen duration was heterogeneous, with 66% receiving FDA/IBCG-defined adequate BCG; treatment heterogeneity may confound disease outcomes; not all pT1 patients underwent re-TURBT, potentially confounding oncologic outcomes in that subgroup.
