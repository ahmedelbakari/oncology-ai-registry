MSIntuit CRC

Owkin
Histopathology colorectal

AI pre-screening for microsatellite instability (MSI) in colorectal cancer from H&E slides.

Regulatory statusResearch Use

Research Use Only (RUO) — not for use in clinical diagnostic procedures.

Validation summary

Study designRetrospective
Cohort size600 patients — 600 consecutive CRC patients diagnosed across 9 pathology laboratories over a 2-year window; blind validation cohort independent of TCGA training data.
Number of sites9
Site geographyMulti-center (international)
ComparatorGold-standard test
Primary endpointsensitivity_specificity
Primary resultSensitivity 0.96–0.98 (across two scanners), Specificity 0.46–0.47, inter-scanner Cohen's κ = 0.82. Specific 95% CIs not captured in summary text — see published paper for exact intervals.
External validationYes — Independent of TCGA training cohort; 600 consecutive CRC cases across 9 different pathology labs over 2 years. · Inter-scanner Cohen's κ = 0.82 demonstrates robust generalization across two scanner platforms.
Peer-reviewedYes

Key publications

  • Validation of MSIntuit as an AI-based pre-screening tool for MSI detection from colorectal cancer histology slides pivotal
    Nature Communications · 2023

Limitations noted

Specificity is intentionally low (~0.47) because MSIntuit is positioned as a pre-screening rule-out tool, not a confirmatory MSI test; positive predictions still require confirmatory MMR-IHC or PCR. Trained on TCGA samples — generalizability to non-TCGA-like populations should be confirmed by external real-world studies.
